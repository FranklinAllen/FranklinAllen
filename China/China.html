China Highlights

I traveled to China as a tourist with the intentions of listening to God's leading. Not being a part of any denomination, I was able to learn quickly in how to survive as a self-sustaining servant of God.

Traveling through mainland China.

During this journey I learned about the many different religions of the world. I developed a passion for understanding the history of religion as I began to share the gospel. God granted me divine appointments that challenged my understanding while driving me to study the scriptures for myself.

My first time preaching was in Hong Kong.

It was here that God led me to become a Seventh-day Adventist. The calling for pastoral ministry was clear. After China I would enroll at Southwestern Adventist University for a bachelors in Theology.

A 27 hour train ride that opened my eyes.

As I was traveling I discovered creative ways to keep overhead costs down. I chose to live out of a backpack while learning to live off of the land. I learned the value of fellowship and the efforts it took to raise funding for the purpose of spreading the gospel.

Freindships that were made along the way that will never be forgotten.

Each person I came in contact with was an oppurtunity to learn and grow. I learned the value of community as I was being established with my trust in Christ.
